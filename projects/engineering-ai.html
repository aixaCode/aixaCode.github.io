Case study

AI for Engineering Teams

Introducing AI into engineering as a quality, knowledge and delivery system rather than a productivity slogan.

AI PR review / QA automation / knowledge assistants / dashboards / workflow design

Internal or private project material.All projects

Problem

Most AI adoption fails because it optimizes for visible activity. The useful question is where AI reduces risk, improves quality or makes organizational knowledge easier to access.

Goals

  • Add AI review into the PR process
  • Use AI to strengthen QA and regression detection
  • Create engineering dashboards and knowledge workflows
  • Reduce dependency on individual memory
  • Keep humans accountable for decisions

Architecture

graph TD
  Developer[Developer] --> PR[Pull request]
  PR --> AIReview[AI review]
  AIReview --> Human[Developer review]
  Human --> QA[QA validation]
  QA --> Release[Release]
  Knowledge[Docs + metrics + decisions] --> Assistant[Knowledge assistant]
  Assistant --> Team[Team answers]

Interesting problems

  • Where should AI sit in the review process? It should catch issues and improve attention, not replace accountable reviewers.
  • How do you avoid AI becoming noise? Trigger it where it changes decisions or catches regressions, and remove it where it only adds ceremony.
  • How do you make knowledge access safer? Source-backed answers and clear uncertainty beat confident summaries.

Lessons learned

  • AI adoption is an operating model change, not a tooling rollout.
  • Quality and knowledge access are better first-order goals than raw speed.
  • The best AI workflows are boring enough to run every week.
